REMEDIATION OF MISCONCEPTION: CONCEPTUAL CHANGE & COGNITIVE CONFLICT

Conceptual clarity is an essential prerequisite for future learning of scientific knowledge. However, misconceptions in science are simultaneously a universal phenomenon among students. It has also been established that students’ misconceptions have multiple sources. It can be due to errors in learning at home, certain cultural beliefs and even classroom instruction.

The misconception has been empirically validated to interfere with students’ future learning. As such it is important that the teachers pay attention to the misconceptions and make efforts to remedy them.

Remediation of misconception demands remedial instructional plans different from conventional plans. It is therefore deemed important that the teachers know, in a simple way, the theoretical background of remediation strategies and the instructional approach based on them.
The present module introduces you to the background knowledge of the phenomenon of conceptual change and cognitive conflict as the theoretical base for the remediation of misconceptions.

Identification of Misconceptions

You have already learned in Module 1 that misconceptions are a general phenomenon and that the teachers' initial role is to identify their students’ misconceptions related to various concepts/ideas in science.

The teacher can identify the misconception related to some particular topic/concept following any of the three strategies or a combination of them:

  • Selecting some predeveloped diagnostic tests.

  • Developing and using the diagnostic test of their concern.

  • Reviewing the literature to identify the major misconception related to the concerned concept.

Remediation: Theoretical Underpinning

Remediation of misconception refers to teachers' instructional approach to remove the error in students' existing conception. The remediation of the misconceptions is explained by the theory of Conceptual Change mediated by a conflict in students' minds called Cognitive Conflict.

The fundamental remedial principle is that any remedial instructional design creates a cognitive conflict among the students and motivates them for conceptual change.

CONCEPTUAL CHANGE

Any remediation process is in essence a process of Conceptual Change.
Conceptual Change is mediated by the state of cognitive conflict created in students' minds which is often followed by instructional facilitation by the teacher for conceptual change.
Conceptual change is about change in the existing conception to match it with the valid and accepted conception. Conceptual change involves the process of change in the student’s existing knowledge/cognitive structure.



The theory of Conceptual Change goes like this:

  • Learning involves interaction with the learning materials.

  • This interaction between the learner and the material of learning is mediated by the student’s existing knowledge popularly termed prior knowledge or previous knowledge.

  • Any systematic error in existing knowledge structure related to some particular idea or concept is referred to as a misconception. It is thus some idea in the student's existing knowledge store related to the target concept different from that accepted as valid by the scientific community.



  • Since learning is mediated by the existing knowledge structure, any error or misconception in the structure hinders interpretation, organization and correct learning of the new material.

  • Since learning is mediated by the existing knowledge structure, any error or misconception in the structure hinders interpretation, organization and correct learning of the new material.

  • Rectification of the error is achieved by restructuring the prior knowledge structure. This restructuring of the prior knowledge involves a change commonly known as Conceptual Change (Schneider et al, 2012). Conceptual Change is thus a phenomenon of change.

Cognitive Conflict:



Till now you have learned that conceptual change is a change in the existing cognitive structure of the students related to some concept, phenomenon or event.
Conceptual change is the essence of any Remedial Instructional strategy vis-a-vis misconception. The conceptual change is achieved through the mediation of a motivational force in the form of cognitive conflict.
Cognitive conflict is a mental state that serves as a motivation to initiate conceptual change. Thus the cognitive conflict is the driver for the process of conceptual change.
Cognitive conflict is a “psychological state” of mind that acts as a driver for the students to engage in the process of conceptual change.
In a nutshell, the state of Cognitive Conflict arises whenever the learner is placed in a conflicting condition where the conflicting condition is an instructional situation in which the learner realizes that the learned concepts in their prior knowledge could not help them interpret some new learning material and it demands restructuring (Vosniadou, 1994; Chi, 2008).



ILLUSTRATION OF (COGNITIVE CONFLICT)


HEAT


Students believe that temperature can be measured by touch. They successfully categorize given objects in increasing order of hotness or coldness.
However, when the student encounters two objects with marginal differences in their temperature, they fail to use their existing knowledge of determining differences in hotness. e. A situation of conflict arises.
The student is motivated to undergo cognitive change to address the discrepancy. Source: Lee et.al.(2003)

COGNITIVE CONFLICT AND CONFLICTING CONDITION

You have learned thus far that Cognitive conflict is the driving force behind the conceptual change. For cognitive conflict to occur in a manageable and teachable way, the teacher needs to design instruction such that it creates a condition in which the students feel the conflict cognitively and desire to overcome the conflict.

Thus there is a logical relationship between cognitive conflict and the conflicting condition/situation as described below:

  • Conceptual change is the essence of Remediation for Misconceptions.

  • Conceptual change is mediated by a particular psychological state of the student. This particular psychological state is termed Cognitive Conflict.

  • The state of Cognitive Conflict among the student is achieved through designing and implementing a conflicting situation in the classroom.

  • The state of Cognitive Conflict among the student is achieved through designing and implementing a conflicting situation in the classroom.

  • The conflicting condition is a deliberately planned classroom-based instructional situation. The situation is such that the student finds some fallacy or shortcoming or error in what they already know.

  • The realization of the fallacy arises when the student fails to interpret some learning material or solve certain problems based on a given concept/idea relevant to the situation.

  • Thus the conflicting conditions are well planned and deliberately created by the teacher to help the learner find their knowledge structure deficient in some respect.
